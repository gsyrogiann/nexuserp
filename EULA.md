# NexusERP End User License Agreement

Draft baseline for internal release-readiness review. This document is not
legal advice and should be finalized by legal review before commercial launch.

## 1. Scope

This End User License Agreement governs use of the NexusERP software, related
documentation, updates, integrations, and hosted or self-managed deployment
components made available under a separate commercial, pilot, partner, or
evaluation agreement.

NexusERP is licensed, not sold.

## 2. License Grant

Subject to payment terms and the applicable order form, pilot agreement, or
service agreement, the customer receives a limited, non-exclusive,
non-transferable, non-sublicensable right to access and use NexusERP for its
internal business operations during the agreed subscription or evaluation term.

## 3. Deployment Model

The effective commercial agreement must state whether the customer receives:

* access to a hosted SaaS deployment;
* rights to run an approved on-prem installation; or
* a hybrid deployment arrangement.

No rights outside the explicitly documented deployment model are granted.

## 4. Restrictions

Unless explicitly allowed by the governing commercial agreement or mandatory
law, the customer must not:

* resell, sublicense, lease, or provide NexusERP to third parties;
* copy or distribute the software beyond permitted backup or operational use;
* reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble the software;
* bypass access controls, license controls, security controls, or usage limits;
* remove proprietary notices or attribution material; or
* use the software in a manner that violates applicable law, privacy duties,
  tax obligations, or third-party platform terms.

## 5. Customer Responsibilities

The customer is responsible for:

* maintaining lawful rights to its own data;
* controlling user access and administrator access;
* protecting credentials, tokens, API keys, and connected service accounts;
* configuring integrations only with authorized external systems; and
* complying with applicable accounting, privacy, telecom, labor, and tax
  regulations relevant to its use of NexusERP.

## 6. Data and Integrations

NexusERP may process customer, supplier, invoicing, communications, and other
business records supplied by the customer. The customer remains responsible for
the accuracy, lawfulness, and retention obligations of that data.

Third-party integrations, including email, telephony, AI, tax, and payment
services, are subject to the availability and terms of those third parties.

## 7. Support and Updates

Support scope, response expectations, maintenance windows, and update cadence
must be defined in the commercial agreement, support policy, or service level
documentation in force at the time of use.

## 8. Security and Incident Handling

NexusERP security reporting and handling expectations are described in
`SECURITY.md`. Hosted deployments should also be governed by operational,
backup, incident response, and support commitments in the applicable service
agreement.

## 9. Warranties

Except to the extent expressly stated in a signed agreement, NexusERP is
provided on an "as is" and "as available" basis without implied warranties of
merchantability, fitness for a particular purpose, non-infringement, or
continuous availability.

## 10. Liability and Indemnity

Commercial terms for liability caps, exclusions, indemnities, data processing,
and service credits must be defined in the governing agreement before GA or
paid rollout.

## 11. Termination

The license terminates automatically if the customer materially breaches the
agreement or uses NexusERP outside the permitted scope. On termination, the
customer must stop using the software and follow the agreed data export and
offboarding process.

## 12. Order of Precedence

If this EULA conflicts with a signed commercial agreement, the signed agreement
controls.
